Text Analysis

For this assignments I analyzed two phenomenal books, Animal Farm by George Orwell and Lord of the Flies written by William Golding and compared interesting features between one another.

In this project I used digital tools like Voyant and AntConc to help me get a deeper view of these books most repeated words and N-Grams to grasp a better understanding of them.

The reason I chose to analyse and compare these two books is because they are very similar and different at the same time. What I mean with this is that the two books share a deep metaphorical message telling the story of the behaviour of a group of characters and how they interact with each other, but they do it in very different styles. Even without reading the books, thanks to the use of AntConc we can tell how characters in both books deal with rules set by themselves and all the controversies these create.

Let’s take a look at Lord of the Flies first, we see the word “Conch” as a symbol of order and respect, the one that has the conch has the word. But the more we move forward in the story we see how they start fighting, questioning and changing that rule that was keeping them civil. And something very similar applies to Animal Farm. The animals set a phrase to help them separate good from evil, “four legs good, two legs bad”. But right at the bottom of the picture AntConc shows us how the phrase transforms into “four legs good, two legs better!”. Basically, they ended up becoming the same thing they were told to hate.

A contrast I really like about these two masterpieces is how animals are seen by the characters in the story. In one they are the heroes and protagonists, and in the other as beasts and as an insult.

Although the correlation is not perfect, the graphics are pretty similar between the words “throat” and “beast” in Lord of the Flies and “hero” and “animal” in Animal Farm. Voyant does an amazing job helping us visualize words' appearances and consistency throughout the story. The graphs are both fantastic representations of the contrast that the two stories have towards animals and the roles they play.

Another very interesting point to compare in these books, are the main characters and how they are perceived by the rest of the characters. In one we have Napoleon, a leader and an authority to the other animals, and in the other we have Ralph, a character who started as the leader but his authority vanishes throughout the story. The way the other characters talk about them and to them says a lot of their actual role in the story. Thankfully we have AntConc and Voyant to see the contrast.

Again, even though the correlation between “hero”, “right” and “Napoleon'' is not perfect we can see similarities in the curves. On the other hand when we look at “Ralph” and the word “right”, the results are very inconsistent and the correlation is practically nonexistent. Both books narrate very different leadership stories and the words that come along with these leaders' names, are a great way to tell.

Lastly I would like to compare the settings where the stories take place. The way the characters perceive the setting says a lot about their well-being and behaviours. For example, if we see the word “farm” and phrases related to it, they are mostly positive, meaning that the animals are comfortable and happy where they are. But if we check “island” in Lord of the Flies, the contexts where this word appears are mostly negative, suggesting that the boys are struggling to survive.

Not only does AntConc show us these results, but if we plug “farm” and “good” in Voyant we can see a strong correlation. Something similar happens, when type “island” and “death” for example, we can also see that the graphs are correlated. Once again, the relationship between some words is indicating very interesting features about the books settings, characters and dialogs.

This type of project is clear example of how many differences and contrasts you can find between two books that are pretty similar in terms of themes and metaphorical background. Like I said at the beginning, the styles in which both stories are narrated is what makes each one unique and memorable. And the best way to see these books in depth is with text analysis tools like AntCon and Voyant.
