Videogame Analysis

Vanish by ZeroDigitZ

Features:

  • - 30 Minute gameplay
  • - Mystery & Horror RPG
  • - Compatible with MacOS and Windows
  • - Original Soundtrack
  • - Puzzles & Minigames

Gameplay:

The gameplay is very simple throughout the game. The movements are forward, backwards, left and right and you can either click with the mouse where you want to go or use the arrows at the bottom right of the keyboard. To move forward in conversations or character interactions you can press the space bar or enter. Selecting options is also pretty easy, you can click on them or just move with the arrows through the options and press enter when you choose it.

Graphics:

The graphics in vanish are what is known as 2D sprite tile-based graphical style, which I think are a great choice for these type of games. Other exploration-adventure games such as Terraria also have these graphics because it makes the map easy to understand. Every asset, NPC or room in Vanish is well designed, there are a lot of colors that make the game more appealing.

Sound:

The videogame includes an original, unique soundtrack that is probably one of the best features. Since the moment you open it a misterious and scary music starts playing and immediately immerse yourself in the game. Everytime you go through a door or something unexpected is happening, like an alarm going off or the floor breaking there is a specific noise in the background. "The Flock" has an actual voice, that seems to be modified digitally to give a more dramatic effect. Click on each one to listen to them.

Vanish Soundtrack:

  • - Safe Room
  • - Breaking Point
  • - Main Theme
  • - Marvelous
  • - The Curse
  • - Headshot
  • - The Flock

Game Options:

  • - Chapter 1: Here is where the story begins, and where you go from being a confused and lost character, to knowing your way around the game and have an idea of how to leave this place.
  • - Chapter 2: The second chapter is more about actually leaving the "prison" and finding a way to do it with character Number 3. Here, you also find who is behind all of these and get closer to the truth of what that place is.
  • - Maze: The Maze game mode is not related to the plot from chapter 1 & 2, and it basically consists of choosing between different doors options until you find your way out.
  • - Escape: This last option, like the Maze, is not connected to the main story of the game. You have to escape different rooms in a certain time by solving clues and puzzles.

NPC's & Objects:

Another great attribute Vanish includes, are interactions with objects around the game. Your character can comment on practically everything he finds, from a toilet, to a book and even to a painting. Some are just nonesense, but some include hidden clues. In regards to the NPC's, the characters you interact with in the game, are very realistic in a way that they react according to what you ask and do. Their dialogs are very connected to the context of the story and really makes the the game captivating.

Main Characters:

  • - #19 (Protagonist): The rookie and protagonist. He is the least experienced out the group which leaves him at a disadvantage, but not necessarily the weakest participant. He plans to do whatever it takes to not only survive, but to go after the individuals who left him imprisoned.
  • - #11: A weird but selfless individual who feels that he has been chosen to compete in the Vanish experiment. Only time will tell if his selflessness will be his downfall.
  • - #3: The veteran of the group. He only cares about survival and will do whatever it takes to escape.
  • - The Flock: A mysterious organization that is at the forefront of the Vanish experiment.
  • - The Supervisor: A high ranking member of the flock. And probably the person behind all of this.

Overall Review:

Although as simple as the game appears to be, it has an excellent and engaging story with very interesting characters. The map is big enough to feel like you are always exploring without loosing sense of where you need to go. The puzzles and minigames put a challenge to the game that really makes you stop for a moment and take your time solving them. There are plot twists and surpises throughout the story which makes those 30 minutes of gameplay feel like much more. The only thing that I would add is a little more inforation at the end of what the place is to help you understand the whole videogame better. But as far as the music, graphics, gameplay and story goes, Vanish is a very enjoyable game.
